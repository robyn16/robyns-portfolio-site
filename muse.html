MUSE

------------------

Print & digital design within the existing MUSE brand

MUSE is a sophisticated quarterly magazine that covers fashion, art, beauty, design, photography, cinema, music and visual culture. Keeping with the magazine's minimalist design aesthetic, I worked with another designer and the creative director, selecting images, designing layouts and customizing the type in feature headings for both print and web. In addition to design, I was heavily involved with copywriting, print production, and working with the team to select appropriate content for our audience.

For this issue, we combined Didot, a modern, neoclassical typeface with Din, a clear sans-serif typeface first developed for wayfinding and technical applications. This contrast of type complemented the magazine's content. The beauty and romance of the photographs were explained and presented to our audience in an accessible and straightforward way.
